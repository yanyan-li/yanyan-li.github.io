Yan Li (李言)

Research Fellow, National University of Singapore · Incoming Assistant Professor, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou)

My research focuses on robotics, spatial AI, SLAM, 3D/4D scene representation, and Gaussian Splatting. I am interested in how robots can perceive the physical world, build persistent internal models, understand geometry and dynamics, and act intelligently over long horizons. I received my training at Peking University (PKU) and the Technical University of Munich (TUM), where I worked on bundle adjustment, online camera tracking, scene reconstruction, and geometry-aware scene understanding.

My long-term goal is to build next-generation intelligent robots from body to mind, combining robot hardware, spatial understanding, persistent world modeling, and robot action policy learning.

I am actively recruiting motivated PhD and MPhil students, research assistants, and interns to join Embedded Spatial AI Lab at HKUST(GZ).

About

Research Vision

I study how robots can localize, reconstruct, understand, and interact with the world through structured, geometry-aware, and temporally consistent scene representations.

A central theme of my research is persistent spatial intelligence: enabling embodied agents to perceive the world, build internal representations, reason over geometry and dynamics, and support intelligent decision-making and action in real-world environments.

Research

Research Areas

My work lies at the intersection of robotics, computer vision, spatial AI, scene representation, and embodied learning.

Embodied Spatial Perception

Spatial perception for robots in real-world environments, including localization, mapping, geometry, and scene understanding.

3D / 4D Scene Representation

Persistent scene representations for static and dynamic worlds, spanning reconstruction, rendering, Gaussian Splatting, and world modeling.

SLAM and World Models

Geometry-centric tracking, mapping, optimization, and structured spatial representations for long-horizon robot autonomy.

Embodied AI and Robot Learning

Vision-language-action, policy learning, and intelligent robot systems that connect perception, representation, reasoning, and action.
